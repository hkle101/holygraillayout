Common Cyber Attacks

Explore common types of attacks and practical defenses — phishing, injection attacks, and denial-of-service are covered below. Click each box title to learn more about each attack and how to protect yourself.

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing relies on deceptive messages that trick people into revealing credentials or installing malware. Social engineering extends this to phone calls or other manipulations. Look for suspicious senders, verify requests out-of-band, and never share passwords over email.

Injection Attacks

Injection (for example SQL or command injection) exploits unvalidated inputs to run unintended commands on a server. Prevent these with input validation, prepared statements/parameterized queries, and strict escaping.

Denial of Service (DoS)

DoS and DDoS attacks flood services with traffic to make them unavailable. Mitigations include rate limiting, CDN/waf protection, and scalable infrastructure to absorb spikes.
